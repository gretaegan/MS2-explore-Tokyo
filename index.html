Where to Visit ?

Shinjuku

Shinjuku is the nightlife capital of Tokyo. Known for its bright, neon lights, Shinjuku is home to thousands of bars and restaurants. Some mega-clubs and some only big enough for a handful of patrons. This exciting mini-city has something for everyone.

Asakusa

Home to the magnificent Senso-Ji temple, Asakusa is a neighbourhood that's full of traditional Japanese style. Situated in Taito, Asakusa is the place to go for traditional Japanese food.

Shibuya

Shibuya is most widely known for its shopping, Meiji-Jingu shrine and the famous Shibuya crossing. Stroll down Takeshita street in Harajuku and take in the colourful street style of young Japanese, take a walk through the magnificent gardens and shrine of Meiji-Jingu or indulge in some of Tokyo's best shopping malls.
